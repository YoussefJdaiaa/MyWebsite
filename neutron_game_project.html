Project Details

Development of a program for the neutron game. Creation of an interactive platform. Application of game modeling concepts in C.

Game Instructions

The neutron game is a strategy game for two players. The goal is to move the neutron (N) to the first or last row of the board, or to block the opponent so they can no longer make a move. The red pieces (R) are controlled by one player and the blue pieces (B) by the other player. The neutron can be moved by both players during their turn.

How to play:

  • Each player takes turns selecting one of their pieces or the neutron by clicking on it.
  • Once selected, the player chooses a direction (from 1 to 8) to move the piece.
  • The piece moves in the chosen direction until it encounters an obstacle or the edge of the board.
  • The game continues until a player reaches the first or last row with the neutron or blocks the opponent.

Example of movement:

Suppose you control the blue pieces (B) and it is your turn to play. You click on one of your blue pieces, for example, the one at position (5, 1). Then, a dialog box opens asking you to choose a direction. The directions are numbered from 1 to 8 as follows:

  • 1 : Down (↓) - [1, 0] in matrix (adds 1 to the row)
  • 2 : Up (↑) - [-1, 0] in matrix (subtracts 1 from the row)
  • 3 : Right (→) - [0, 1] in matrix (adds 1 to the column)
  • 4 : Left (←) - [0, -1] in matrix (subtracts 1 from the column)
  • 5 : Down-Right (↘) - [1, 1] in matrix (adds 1 to the row and the column)
  • 6 : Up-Right (↗) - [-1, 1] in matrix (subtracts 1 from the row and adds 1 to the column)
  • 7 : Up-Left (↖) - [-1, -1] in matrix (subtracts 1 from the row and the column)
  • 8 : Down-Left (↙) - [1, -1] in matrix (adds 1 to the row and subtracts 1 from the column)

If you choose direction 3 (Right), the piece will move to the right until it encounters an obstacle or the edge of the board. For example, if there is no obstacle, the piece at (5, 1) will move to (5, 5).
